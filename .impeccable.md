# Impeccable - Design Context

Used by the `impeccable` skill suite (craft, teach, audit, polish, critique, etc.) to avoid generic AI aesthetics. Every design decision should trace back to something in this file.

## Design Context

### Users

Marketing leaders and founders at Irish growth brands - typically solo marketers, 1-3 person marketing teams, or agency strategists serving them. Non-technical but data-literate. They open CMO.ie during the working day, in a browser, between Slack and a calendar. They want quick, confident answers about how AI search engines (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, Google AI Overviews) talk about their brand and their competitors - without having to become an SEO analyst to interpret the output.

### Jobs to be done

- "Is my brand being mentioned when customers ask AI about my category?"
- "Where do I rank against competitors on the prompts I care about?"
- "What should I do this month to improve my visibility?"

### Brand Personality

Three words: **confident, considered, dry.**

Adjacent references: The Economist's visual restraint, Aesop's retail typography, Linear's product polish, Stripe's copy confidence. Writing is plain-spoken, never salesy; a little wry. No startup-bro enthusiasm. No emojis. No "growth hacking" vocabulary.

### Aesthetic Direction

- **Theme**: light, paper-warm. Marketers use this in the day, in normal-lit rooms, in normal moods. Dark mode is not a goal.
- **Palette**: warm near-white surfaces, near-black type (never pure white or pure black), **deep forest green** as a rare accent. Primary CTAs are matte black, quiet.
- **No**: gradients on CTAs, gradient text, drop-shadow halos, glowing borders, sparklines as decoration, amber/gold (tried it - too warm, too editorial-finance), cool slate (tried it - too generic SaaS).
- **Yes**: clear type hierarchy, generous line-height, left-aligned ease, restrained use of the forest green for focus rings / selections / hover borders only.

### Design Principles

1. **Quiet confidence over visual noise.** If the information can speak for itself, let it. A table with good type hierarchy beats a card with a chart in it.
2. **Black is the loudest colour we use.** Never combine black with a coloured glow, gradient, or halo - black already carries the weight. Accent colour shows up only on focus rings, selection, and a few hover states.
3. **Tint everything toward the brand green.** Surfaces, borders, text - all warmed by a trace of the forest green hue. No cool-slate neutrals.
4. **Press + stagger are the signature interactions.** Every pressable element scales to 0.97 on `:active`. Lists cascade in with a 40ms stagger. These are the two details people notice without knowing why.
5. **No AI-slop fingerprints.** Banned: `background-clip: text` gradients, side-stripe borders, pulsing glows on cards, DM Sans / Inter / IBM Plex / Fraunces.

### Typography

- **Body + UI**: Geist (Google Fonts, off the reflex list)
- **Mono**: Geist Mono (tabular numbers for metrics)
- Type scale: 1.25 ratio minimum. Favour weight contrast over excessive size range.
- Body width capped at 65-75ch.

### Motion

- Easing: custom `cubic-bezier(0.23, 1, 0.32, 1)` tokenised as `--ease-strong-out`.
- Durations: 100ms press, 150ms colour, 200ms hover lift, 300ms max anywhere in UI.
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` - fade, don't move.
- Never animate layout properties (width, height, padding). Transform + opacity only.

### Accessibility

- WCAG AA minimum. Focus rings are visible and brand-coloured (forest green).
- Reduced-motion honoured: transforms suspended, animations slowed to near-zero, `animate-ping` disabled.
- Touch-device hover gating: consider `@media (hover: hover) and (pointer: fine)` before adding a hover effect to avoid sticky-hover on iOS.

### Anti-references

Do not look like: generic Shadcn starter, Vercel-v0 default output, linear-gradient landing pages, glowing purple AI SaaS, cards-with-icons-above-headings marketing pages.
