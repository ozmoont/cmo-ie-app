# Phase 7 scope — Audit Council

_Target window: 2-3 weeks after Phase 6 settles in production._
_Status as of 28 April 2026: design only. Not yet started._

## Why Phase 7 matters

Every customer-facing artifact CMO.ie ships is generated by a single LLM call, often without grounded sources. That's been fine for prototyping, but it's the highest hallucination surface in the product:

- The brand profile incident (commit `862555e`) shipped a digital-agency profile to a non-agency brand because we'd let the model guess from the brand name alone. That class of error is invisible to the customer until they read the result, and corrosive to trust when they do.
- SEO audits make dozens of factual claims per run — competitor names, market segments, schema-markup recommendations, Core Web Vitals interpretations. One wrong claim per audit is enough to sink a sales conversation.
- Monthly playbooks are highly visible, low-frequency, big-stakes artifacts. A bad playbook is a churn driver.
- Phase 6 prompt batches will mint 30-50 prompts per click; an industry-lock violation in any one of them poisons the tracking dataset.

The pattern that catches these is **cross-model verification**. If three frontier LLMs from different vendors independently flag the same statement as suspicious, the probability that all three are wrong about the same thing in the same way is vanishingly small. Conversely, when all three approve, you've reduced your false-negative rate by an order of magnitude.

Phase 7 wires up that pattern as an **Audit Council** — three "senior auditor" instances (Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini), each reviewing every generated artifact against the same rubric, with a chair model synthesising their reports for a CMO.ie ops reviewer. The customer never sees any of this. They get the same artifact they get today; the ops team gets a private feed of approvals, flags, and disagreements they can use to spot regressions early.

## Success criteria

- Every generated artifact in scope (see § Scope) automatically triggers a council review on completion. No customer-facing latency added — the council runs after `NextResponse.json` returns.
- An admin-only dashboard at `/admin/audit-council` shows every review with: artifact preview, each auditor's verdict + rationale, the chair's synthesis, and an ops decision panel.
- Council runs in **observation mode for v1** — no auto-blocking, no auto-regeneration, no Slack alerts. We log everything and let the ops team build muscle memory before deciding what to gate.
- Aggregate metrics on the dashboard: rolling 30-day flag rate by artifact type, auditor agreement rate, time-to-ops-decision.
- One full review costs ≤ $0.40 across the three auditor calls + chair synthesis. Monthly cost at projected volume stays under $400.
- The customer-facing UI is **completely unchanged**. Zero new components on customer surfaces. No env-flagged "experimental" badges. Hidden means hidden.

## Out of scope (deferred)

- **Auto-gating the customer surface.** Don't hold artifacts in pending until human approval. We'll only consider this once we have 4+ weeks of observation data showing the council can reliably distinguish real flags from noise.
- **Auto-regeneration.** Tempting but risky — feeding the model its own flag rationale produces unstable outputs. Park until v2.
- **Slack / email alerting.** Easy to wire later; intentionally left off for v1 to avoid alert fatigue before we know the baseline flag rate.
- **Auditor self-improvement.** A "judge the auditors" step where we mark which auditor was right when they disagree, then use that signal to weight future verdicts. Interesting; deferred.
- **Customer-visible "verified by council" badge.** Marketing once asked about this. Out of scope until we trust the council enough to gate.

## Architecture

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  Generator routes (Phase 1-6 unchanged)                             │
│  /api/projects/[id]/seo-audits, /actions/draft, /actions/brief, …   │
│  /api/prompts/generate, /api/projects/[id]/profile/regenerate, …    │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
                │
                │  on success, fire enqueueAuditReview(artifactType, id)
                │  via Next.js after() — non-blocking; customer's response
                │  is already on the wire.
                ▼
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  src/lib/audit-council/council.ts                                   │
│  • Loads the artifact's full content                                │
│  • Runs three auditors in parallel:                                 │
│      Senior Auditor (Claude) — Sonnet 4.6                          │
│      Senior Auditor (ChatGPT) — GPT-4.1                            │
│      Senior Auditor (Gemini) — Gemini 2.5 Pro                      │
│  • Runs the Chair synthesiser (Haiku) over the three reports       │
│  • Persists everything in audit_reviews                             │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
                │
                ▼
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  /admin/audit-council  (CMO.ie ops-only)                            │
│  Inbox: pending → flagged → approved-with-caveats → approved        │
│  Drill-down: artifact preview + each auditor's verdict + chair      │
│  Aggregate: flag rate / agreement rate / ops-decision time          │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

The council itself is shaped as one entry function, three parallel auditor functions, one synthesiser. Failures in any single auditor are non-fatal — the chair still runs over the surviving reports and notes which auditor errored.

### Sampling

Some artifacts are too high-frequency to audit at 100% within budget. v1 sampling rates:

| Artifact | Audit rate | Why |
|---|---|---|
| SEO audit | 100% | Low-frequency, highest-stakes |
| Monthly playbook | 100% | Low-frequency, customer-visible |
| Brand profile | 100% | Already had one hallucination incident; small artifact, cheap |
| Phase 6 prompt batch | 100% | Industry-lock violations contaminate tracking |
| Action plan | 20% | Highest frequency; sample for statistical signal |
| Brief | 20% | Highest frequency; sample for statistical signal |

Sampling is a deterministic hash on `artifact_id` so the same artifact always gets the same decision (idempotent re-trigger) and we hit the same 20% bucket reproducibly.

## Schema impact

New migration: `025_audit_council.sql`. Adds one table.

```sql
CREATE TABLE public.audit_reviews (
  id UUID PRIMARY KEY DEFAULT gen_random_uuid(),

  -- ── What's being audited ────────────────────────────────
  artifact_type TEXT NOT NULL CHECK (artifact_type IN (
    'seo_audit', 'monthly_playbook', 'action_plan', 'brief',
    'brand_profile', 'prompt_batch'
  )),
  artifact_id UUID NOT NULL,
  org_id UUID NOT NULL REFERENCES public.organisations(id) ON DELETE CASCADE,
  project_id UUID REFERENCES public.projects(id) ON DELETE CASCADE,

  -- ── Council state ──────────────────────────────────────
  status TEXT NOT NULL DEFAULT 'pending'
    CHECK (status IN ('pending', 'running', 'complete', 'error')),
  /* Whether this review came in because of sampling or full
     coverage. Lets us compare flag rates between sampled +
     fully-covered artifacts when tuning rates later. */
  sampled BOOLEAN NOT NULL DEFAULT FALSE,

  -- ── Per-auditor reports (full structured JSON) ─────────
  claude_report JSONB,
  chatgpt_report JSONB,
  gemini_report JSONB,

  -- ── Chair synthesis ────────────────────────────────────
  chair_verdict TEXT
    CHECK (chair_verdict IN ('approve', 'approve_with_caveats', 'flag', 'fail')),
  chair_summary TEXT,
  /* Per-auditor agreement on chair_verdict. NULL until chair runs. */
  agreement_score NUMERIC(3, 2),

  -- ── Telemetry ──────────────────────────────────────────
  cost_usd NUMERIC(10, 4),
  duration_ms INTEGER,
  error_message TEXT,

  -- ── Ops decision ───────────────────────────────────────
  ops_decision TEXT
    CHECK (ops_decision IN ('approved', 'overridden', 'mark_regenerate')),
  ops_decision_at TIMESTAMP WITH TIME ZONE,
  ops_decision_by UUID REFERENCES auth.users(id),
  ops_notes TEXT,

  created_at TIMESTAMP WITH TIME ZONE NOT NULL DEFAULT NOW(),
  completed_at TIMESTAMP WITH TIME ZONE
);

CREATE INDEX idx_audit_reviews_artifact
  ON public.audit_reviews(artifact_type, artifact_id);
CREATE INDEX idx_audit_reviews_pending
  ON public.audit_reviews(status, created_at DESC)
  WHERE status IN ('pending', 'running');
CREATE INDEX idx_audit_reviews_verdict
  ON public.audit_reviews(chair_verdict, created_at DESC)
  WHERE chair_verdict IS NOT NULL;
CREATE INDEX idx_audit_reviews_undecided
  ON public.audit_reviews(created_at DESC)
  WHERE chair_verdict IS NOT NULL AND ops_decision IS NULL;

ALTER TABLE public.audit_reviews ENABLE ROW LEVEL SECURITY;
-- No public policies. All access via service-role from /api/admin/*
-- (admin-only RLS on the routes).
```

The schema deliberately stores per-auditor reports as JSONB rather than denormalising every issue. Issues are nested + variable-length; the admin UI renders them by reading the JSON.

### Auditor contract

Every auditor — regardless of vendor — returns the same shape:

```ts
export interface AuditorReport {
  auditor: "claude" | "chatgpt" | "gemini";
  model: string; // exact model string for telemetry
  verdict: "approve" | "approve_with_caveats" | "flag" | "fail";
  confidence: number; // 0-1
  issues: AuditIssue[];
  overall_rationale: string;
  /** Cost + tokens for the audit dashboard. */
  usage: { input_tokens: number; output_tokens: number; duration_ms: number };
}

export interface AuditIssue {
  severity: "low" | "medium" | "high";
  category:
    | "factual"          // claim contradicts known fact
    | "industry_lock"    // wrong industry / segment / audience
    | "specificity"      // vague filler instead of concrete advice
    | "consistency"      // contradicts earlier section of the same artifact
    | "citation"         // missing or incorrect source
    | "date"             // stale or anachronistic reference
    | "scope"            // doesn't address the user's actual question
    | "brand_voice"      // off-tone for the brand
    | "other";
  /** ≤ 200 chars; the offending text quoted verbatim. */
  quote: string;
  rationale: string;
  suggested_fix?: string;
}
```

The shared system prompt is in `prompts.ts` and includes:

- Hard rules: no hedging in the verdict; cite specific text in `quote`; one issue = one bullet; never fabricate quotes.
- Per-artifact rubric: SEO audits get a different evaluation rubric than brand profiles get a different rubric than action plans. Lives in `prompts.ts` as `RUBRICS[artifact_type]`.
- Output contract: strict JSON, no fences, matching `AuditorReport` shape.

### Chair contract

The chair takes 1-3 auditor reports (some may have errored) and produces:

```ts
export interface ChairVerdict {
  verdict: "approve" | "approve_with_caveats" | "flag" | "fail";
  summary: string; // ≤ 400 chars; what to tell ops in the inbox row
  agreement_score: number; // 0-1; how much the auditors agreed
  high_severity_issues: AuditIssue[]; // pulled from any auditor's report
  /** Issues every auditor flagged. Strongest signal of a real problem. */
  consensus_issues: AuditIssue[];
}
```

Chair runs on Haiku (~$0.005 per call) so the synthesiser layer is functionally free.

### Public entry: `enqueueAuditReview`

```ts
export function enqueueAuditReview(input: {
  artifactType: AuditedArtifactType;
  artifactId: string;
  orgId: string;
  projectId?: string;
}): void;
```

Generators call this from inside an `after()` block, so it never blocks the customer's response. Internally:

1. Roll the sampling decision. If skipped, exit silently.
2. Insert an `audit_reviews` row in `pending` status.
3. Kick off `runCouncil(reviewId)` — the orchestrator. Errors here are caught and persisted on the row (`status='error'`, `error_message`).

## API contracts

All under `/api/admin/audit-council/*`, admin-RLS-gated (existing `/api/admin/*` pattern).

### `GET /api/admin/audit-council/reviews`

```ts
query: {
  status?: "pending" | "complete" | "error";
  verdict?: "approve" | "approve_with_caveats" | "flag" | "fail";
  artifact_type?: AuditedArtifactType;
  org_id?: string;
  has_ops_decision?: boolean;
  limit?: number;
  offset?: number;
}
```

Returns a paged list. Default sort: `created_at DESC`.

### `GET /api/admin/audit-council/reviews/[id]`

Returns one review with all three auditor reports, the chair verdict, and the underlying artifact (resolved by `artifact_type` + `artifact_id` so the admin UI can render side-by-side).

### `POST /api/admin/audit-council/reviews/[id]/decide`

```ts
body: {
  decision: "approved" | "overridden" | "mark_regenerate";
  notes?: string;
}
```

Records the ops decision. `mark_regenerate` doesn't actually regenerate (deferred to v2); it just flags the row so the operator who fixes the underlying generator can find related reviews.

### `POST /api/admin/audit-council/trigger`

```ts
body: { artifactType: AuditedArtifactType; artifactId: string }
```

Manually trigger a review. For backfills + the case where a generator fired before Phase 7 shipped.

## Admin UI

Lives under `/admin/audit-council`. Keep the existing `/admin/*` editorial style (the dashboard at `/admin` already has tabs for Skills, Playbooks, AI usage, etc.; this is one more).

### Inbox view (`/admin/audit-council`)

- Tabs: **All** · **Pending decision** (chair_verdict ∈ flag | approve_with_caveats, ops_decision IS NULL) · **Flagged** · **Approved** · **Errored**.
- Top-level summary strip: 30-day rolling flag rate, auditor-agreement rate, average ops-decision time.
- Inbox rows: artifact type icon, brand name, chair_verdict badge, top issue snippet, time since review, "Review" button.
- Default sort: pending decisions first, then most recent.

### Single-review view (`/admin/audit-council/[id]`)

Three-column layout:

1. **Artifact preview** — left column. Renders the underlying artifact (markdown via `MarkdownReport` for SEO audits / playbooks / briefs; JSON pretty-print for brand profiles + prompt batches).
2. **Auditor verdicts** — middle column. Three collapsible cards, one per auditor. Each card shows verdict badge, confidence, rationale, list of issues (severity + quote + rationale). Vendor logo on each card so the eye picks out which model said what.
3. **Chair synthesis + ops decision** — right column. Chair summary at top, consensus issues below, then the ops decision panel: approve / override / mark for regeneration buttons + notes textarea.

Disagreement highlighting: where one auditor flagged something and another approved it, that issue gets a "split decision" badge — these are the most interesting rows in the data.

### Aggregate metrics (`/admin/audit-council/metrics`)

- 30-day flag rate by artifact type, with sparkline.
- Auditor agreement matrix (pairwise agreement %).
- Issue category histogram (factual vs industry_lock vs specificity etc.).
- Median ops-decision time by verdict.

## Open questions / future work

- **Auditor weighting.** Right now all three auditors carry equal weight. Once we have ops-decision data, we can compute per-auditor accuracy (how often the auditor's flag was confirmed vs overridden) and start weighting. Don't ship in v1 — risk of overfitting to early decisions.
- **Adversarial auditor prompts.** Some research suggests asking auditors to "argue why this is wrong" (red-team prompt) instead of "evaluate this" surfaces more issues. Worth A/B testing against the neutral evaluator prompt once we have a baseline.
- **Customer-visible "verified" badge.** Marketing has asked. Off the table until we trust the council to gate.
- **Real grounding.** Right now the auditors evaluate from their training data. For factual claims, real grounding (web_search at audit time) would be stronger. Defer until v2; cost would roughly double.
- **Per-org policy.** Some agency customers might want stricter auditing on their playbooks (or skip auditing entirely on early-stage drafts). Out of scope for v1; revisit when an agency asks.
- **Budget circuit breaker.** Hard cap on monthly audit spend; if hit, sampling rates drop to 5% across the board. Worth adding before we scale to thousands of orgs, but premature today.
